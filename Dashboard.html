Why Participate?

  • Sharpen Skills: Hone your coding and design abilities in a challenging yet fun environment.
  • Creative Expression: Showcase your design prowess in the Design Sprint category.
  • Networking: Connect with like-minded students and professionals in the tech and design fields.
  • Prizes: Stand a chance to win exciting prizes and recognition for your achievements.

Rules And Details

The competition will be held online throughout the World (Primary/Upper Elementary & Secondary/Middle School & High School) during the November 27th 2023-15th December, 2023

  • Challenges will be different for each level (Primary/Upper Elementary & Secondary/Middle School) and suitable for the relevant level.
  • Challenges will consist of Basic coding knowledge, Repeat loops, Variables and Arrays.
  • Awards will be given separately for each category (Primary/Upper Elementary & Secondary/Middle School).
  • The project that collects the most stars by completing will be the winner.
  • In the event of equality for code efficiency and the number of stars, time will be taken into account. In this case, the student that finishes in the shortest amount of time will be the winner.

Guidelines

  • Entry must include a block-based project for Primary and Middle grade, and a text-based or block-based project for Secondary Students to qualify.
  • Each entry must include a clear photograph of the participant.
  • Only one submission is allowed per entrant.
  • Each entry must provide parent/guardian or teacher consent and contact information (if under 18 years old).

Objective

Students must design and develop their own original game with a clear objective on a browser-based platform (e.g. Scratch, Game Lab, p5.js, MakeCode Arcade, Buildbox, Construct 3, etc.)

Guidelines

  • Game must be developed by a computer program using browser-based coding environments.
  • The game should allow the user to interact with the game, either through a keyboard or by clicking on the screen.
  • We allow gifs, jpgs, pngs, images from any software image library (e.g. Sprites and backdrops from Scratch’s sprite library), and other graphic files.
  • Games that do not work according to the game’s instructions (if any) will be disqualified. Scratch remixes of other games not created by the coder themself will be disqualified.
  • There is no limit to the number of submissions per contestant, but each contestant may only win one prize per competition category.
  • Each entry must provide parent/guardian or teacher consent and contact information (if under 18 years old).

Objective

Students must design flyers and logos with a clear objective on a browser-based platform (e.g. Photopea, Canva, Adobe Spark, etc.)

Guidelines

  • Designs must be developed by a computer program using browser-based coding environments.
  • The flyers and logo should be original and professional.
  • Designs that do not work according to the instructions (if any) will be disqualified. Flyer and Logo remixes not created by the designer themself will be disqualified.
  • There is no limit to the number of submissions per contestant, but each contestant may only win one prize per competition category.
  • Each entry must provide parent/guardian or teacher consent and contact information (if under 18 years old).

Objective

Students must design 3D artworks with a clear objective on a browser-based platform (e.g. TinkerCAD, BlocksCAD, etc.)

Guidelines

  • Designs must be developed by a computer program using browser-based coding environments.
  • The designs should be original and professional.
  • Designs that do not work according to the instructions (if any) will be disqualified. Remixes not created by the designer themself will be disqualified.
  • There is no limit to the number of submissions per contestant, but each contestant may only win one prize per competition category.
  • Each entry must provide parent/guardian or teacher consent and contact information (if under 18 years old).
